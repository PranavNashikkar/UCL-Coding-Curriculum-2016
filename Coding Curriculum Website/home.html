Processing Physics

Hey and welcome to Processing Physics! In this guide, we'll be teaching you a bit about Java, Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) and physics.

What you'll achieve:

If you follow the project through, you’ll end up with a configurable physics simulation that covers a broad range of physics concepts (gravity, friction and collisions and more). By having a visual simulation, you’ll be able to see the effects of your programming more clearly, and get to see what happens when you experiment with variables. It’ll also help you to develop a more intuitive understanding of the physics concepts, and you’ll even be able to simulate things that would be very hard in reality (ever wanted to double gravity?). You’ll learn Java, and the design principles of ‘Object-Oriented Programming’ which is one of the most used paradigms of programming right now.

Who's this for?

Anyone who has an interest in learning to program, using Processing or Java, and learning about physics.

What do I need to get started?

You'll need to download the JRE available here. You'll also need to download the latest version of 'Procesing'.
